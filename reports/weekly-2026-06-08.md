## Research questions — best-effort answers

### Group 1 — Performance

- **Q1.1 — AI condition → scorePerHit.** Status 🟡 (insufficient n). Finding: Kruskal–Wallis
  H=3.29, df=7. NONE (6.75) is ~1.2 pts/hit lower than AI-advice conditions (range 7.52–9.23),
  consistent with AI advice helping performance regardless of quality. However, NONE has the
  highest mean skill (60 vs 10–40 for other conditions), likely suppressing the NONE score.
  Confidence: Very low (≤6 sessions/cell, skill confound, within-subjects design unmodeled).

- **Q1.2 — Trajectory 5 vs 6 (HEADLINE).** Status 🟡 (very small n). Finding: PLAUSIBLE_GOOD
  (start bad, improve) nominally outscores GOOD_PLAUSIBLE (start good, decline): 9.01 vs 7.52
  pts/hit, Cohen's d=−2.33 (95% CI [−4.63, −0.03], n=2 vs n=3). Direction suggests that
  advice improving over the session (PLAUSIBLE→GOOD) may be better for performance than advice
  declining (GOOD→PLAUSIBLE). However, d=−2.33 from n=5 is not interpretable — the CI barely
  excludes zero and would flip with a single additional session. Confidence: Negligible.

- **Q1.3 — AI → proxOptimal.** Status 🟡 (small n). Finding: CORRECT has lowest proxOptimal
  (10.2 px, identical to proxAI — participants aim exactly at AI when it is correct). NONE
  is highest (49.6 px). RANDOM (37.7 px) and WRONG (24.8 px) are intermediate. Confidence: Low.

- **Q1.4 — satisfied calibrated to scorePerHit.** Status 🟢 (exploratory). Finding: Spearman
  r=+0.58 (n=24). Higher-scoring sessions are associated with higher satisfaction. The direction
  is plausible; within-person structure not yet disentangled. Confidence: Low.

### Group 2 — Trust & influence

- **Q2.1 — condition → trust/influence.** Status 🟡 (small n). Finding: Trust means: CORRECT
  4.17, PLAUSIBLE_GOOD 4.50, NONE 3.20, RANDOM 3.25, GOOD_PLAUSIBLE 3.00, WRONG 2.67, BAD 2.00.
  Broadly: accurate or improving advice → higher trust; misleading/worsening advice → lower trust.
  Influence mirrors trust (r=+0.95). Confidence: Low (1–6 sessions/cell, 2 participants).

- **Q2.2 — trust → proxAI (excl. NONE).** Status 🟢 (exploratory). Finding: Spearman r=−0.45
  (n=19). More trust → throwing closer to AI suggestion. Expected direction confirmed.
  Confidence: Low (n=19, 2 participants; between-condition variance dominates).

- **Q2.3 — trust trajectory 5 vs 6.** Status 🟡 (unchanged). Finding: GOOD_PLAUSIBLE trust=3.00
  (n=3), PLAUSIBLE_GOOD trust=4.50 (n=2). Nominally, participants feel more trust when advice
  improves over the session. Cannot assess intra-session arc (once-per-session survey design).
  Confidence: n/a (n too small).

### Group 3 — Luck

- **Q3.1 — luck vs dispersion/scorePerHit.** Status 🟢 (exploratory). Finding: luck ↔
  scorePerHit r=+0.32 (n=24); luck ↔ dispersion r=−0.35 (n=24). Weak positive links — higher

- **Q3.2 — evGap ↔ luck.** Status 🔴 blocked: flat 8/hit EV placeholder. evGap = scorePerHit − 8

### Group 4 — Mediation & structure

- **Q4.1 — trust mediates condition → performance.** Status 🟡 (unchanged). Finding: pairwise

- **Q4.2 — overall cross-variable structure.** Status 🟢 exploratory. Finding: Three clusters
