## Research questions — best-effort answers

### Group 1 — Performance

- **Q1.1 — AI condition → scorePerHit.** Status 🟡 (descriptively clear, causally confounded).
  Finding: Kruskal–Wallis H=52.97, df=7, p<0.001 (chi-sq approx). Strong evidence that
  scorePerHit differs across conditions. By-condition means (skill-unadjusted): BAD=9.15,
  PG=8.92, RANDOM=8.30, WRONG=8.03, CORRECT=7.70, NONE=7.19, GP=7.27. However, execution_skill
  is severely imbalanced across conditions (means span 10–52.5) and is the primary score driver
  (via dispersion). **The KW result cannot be interpreted as an AI effect** without controlling
  for skill. Confidence: Descriptive pattern is real; causal interpretation blocked by C3.

- **Q1.2 — Trajectory 5 vs 6 (HEADLINE).** Status 🟡 (large nominal effect, critical C3
  confound). Finding: PLAUSIBLE_GOOD (n=26): 8.92 ±0.15 pts/hit. GOOD_PLAUSIBLE (n=26):
  7.27 ±0.24 pts/hit. Difference: +1.65 pts/hit. Cohen's d=−3.16 (95% CI [−3.97, −2.34]).
  However: PLAUSIBLE_GOOD mean skill=15, GOOD_PLAUSIBLE mean skill=45 — a 30-point gap.
  Lower skill produces tighter throws (PG dispersion=13.3 px vs GP=37.9 px) and lower proxOptimal
  (PG=10.9 px vs GP=48.2 px). Trust and following-behavior also differ strongly (PG trust=3.50,
  proxAI=14.5 vs GP trust=2.54, proxAI=60.2), consistent with improving-advice trajectory
  building trust. The score difference is plausibly a skill artifact; the trust and following
  differences are more likely genuine trajectory effects. A mixed model controlling for skill is
  required before the score contrast is interpretable. Confidence: Nominal effect is extremely
  large and consistent, but skill confound is so strong that the score comparison cannot be taken
  at face value without covariate adjustment.

- **Q1.3 — AI → proxOptimal.** Status 🟡 (descriptive pattern clear, skill confound). Finding:
  CORRECT has lowest proxOptimal after PG (37.0 px), PG=10.9 px (likely skill-driven), NONE is
  highest (58.5 px). RANDOM/WRONG are intermediate (38–40 px). The expected ordering — good
  advice moves aim toward optimal — is visible but cannot be separated from skill effects at this
  stage. Confidence: Low; skill covariate needed.

- **Q1.4 — satisfied calibrated to scorePerHit.** Status 🟢 (exploratory). Finding: Spearman
  r=+0.26 (n=260). Positive but modest — higher-scoring sessions correlate with higher satisfaction.
  The moderate correlation (weakened from r=+0.58 in the 24-session pilot) suggests satisfaction
  is not a pure performance proxy; condition assignment and AI interaction quality contribute
  independently. Confidence: Moderate at n=260; within-person mixed model would sharpen this.

### Group 2 — Trust & influence

- **Q2.1 — condition → trust/influence.** Status 🟡 (descriptive pattern plausible). Finding:
  Trust by condition: PLAUSIBLE_GOOD=3.50 > CORRECT=3.25 > NONE=2.67 > RANDOM=2.59 > WRONG=2.56 >
  GOOD_PLAUSIBLE=2.54 > BAD=1.54. Improving advice trajectory (PG) generates highest trust;
  misleading/worsening conditions (BAD, WRONG, GP) generate lowest trust. Influence mirrors trust
  (r=+0.83). The pattern is directionally consistent with the manipulation, but within-subjects
  structure and skill confound should be modeled. Confidence: Moderate (n=13–65 per cell).

- **Q2.2 — trust → proxAI (excl. NONE).** Status 🟢 (exploratory, n=208). Finding: Spearman
  r=−0.36 (n=208). More trust → throwing closer to AI suggestion. Direction confirmed; effect is
  moderate. Importantly, PLAUSIBLE_GOOD shows the highest trust and closest AI following (proxAI
  =14.5 px), consistent with trust driving behavior. RANDOM/BAD show lowest trust and farthest
  from AI (proxAI=233/192 px). Confidence: Moderate; between-condition variance dominates the
  correlation; within-condition mixed model would test the behavioral mediation more cleanly.

- **Q2.3 — trust trajectory 5 vs 6.** Status 🟡 (unchanged design constraint). Finding: PG
  end-of-session trust=3.50 (n=26), GP end-of-session trust=2.54 (n=26). The trajectory
  contrast is now clearer with more sessions: improving advice (PG) generates ~1 point higher
  end-session trust than declining advice (GP), and PG participants follow the AI closely
  (proxAI=14.5 px) while GP participants diverge (60.2 px). Cannot assess intra-session arc
  without intra-session survey probes. Confidence: Moderate for end-state comparison; n=26 per
  arm is interpretable.

### Group 3 — Luck

- **Q3.1 — luck vs dispersion/scorePerHit.** Status 🟢 (exploratory). Finding: luck ↔
  scorePerHit r=+0.095 (n=260); luck ↔ dispersion r=−0.096 (n=260). Very weak correlations —
  subjective luck ratings are nearly uncorrelated with both objective performance and throw
  precision. Luck ratings are narrowly distributed (all conditions: 2.74–3.23/5, little spread),
  suggesting participants mostly attribute outcomes to skill, not luck. Confidence: Moderate at
  n=260; small effect sizes likely real.

- **Q3.2 — evGap ↔ luck.** Status 🟡 (EV grids confirmed, weak signal). Finding: Real EV grids
  are now in use (276 `.bin` files in `public/ev_grids/`). Spearman r(evGap, luck)=−0.035
  (n=260). The EV gap does not significantly predict subjective luck — participants' luck
  attributions are uncorrelated with how much they exceeded or fell short of the aiming location's
  expected value. This is itself an interesting null result: objective luck (evGap) and subjective
  luck are not the same construct. Confidence: Moderate for the null; EV grid correctness should
  be independently validated before strong conclusions.

### Group 4 — Mediation & structure

- **Q4.1 — trust mediates condition → performance.** Status 🟡 (pairwise pieces present, formal
  test still pending). Finding: The three legs of the mediation chain are observable: (a)
  condition affects trust (Q2.1); (b) trust correlates with proxAI (r=−0.36) but not scorePerHit
  (r=−0.02); (c) score differences across conditions are confounded with skill. The near-zero
  trust ↔ scorePerHit correlation (r=−0.02) suggests trust does not mediate score directly —
  consistent with trust influencing *where* one aims (proxAI) but skill/dispersion being the
  dominant score driver. Formal mediation testing requires skill-controlled models and lagged
  trust specifications. Confidence: Low; descriptive legs only.

- **Q4.2 — overall cross-variable structure.** Status 🟢 exploratory. Finding: Three clusters
  remain stable vs the pilot: (a) trust+influence (r=+0.834, collinear self-report items);
  (b) scorePerHit+dispersion (r=−0.986, near-identity cluster — dispersion IS the score
  mechanism); (c) proxAI+proxOptimal (r=+0.414, spatial aim cluster). evGap now loads between
  clusters (b) and (c): r(evGap, dispersion)=+0.789 and r(evGap, scorePerHit)=−0.745, confirming
  it captures something distinct from raw score — how much the actual outcome departs from
  expected given the aim point. luck is structurally isolated (all |r| < 0.15), consistent with
  Q3.1/Q3.2 null results. Confidence: Moderate at n=260.
